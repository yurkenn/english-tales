Chapter 4: The Old Clock

The Old Clock

The old clock on the mantle ticked heavily, each second echoing in the silent room. Dust motes danced in the shaft of afternoon sunlight that pierced through the velvet curtains. It was a time of quiet reflection, or so it seemed to the observer.

He adjusted his spectacles and leaned closer to the manuscript. The ink was faded, the parchment brittle, yet the words seemed to burn with a vibrant intensity that defied their age. It wasn't just a story; it was a testament to a forgotten era.

"Time is not a line, but a vast ocean, and we are merely drifting upon its currents."

With a trembling hand, he turned the page. The rustle was loud in the stillness. A sudden gust of wind rattled the windowpane, as if the elements themselves were eager to see what lay ahead. Shadows lengthened across the floorboards, stretching like grasping fingers towards the hearth.

He remembered the first time he had heard the tale. It was a stormy night in November, the fire crackling in the grate, his grandfather's voice low and rumbling. Now, decades later, the truth was finally within his grasp.

The clock chimed five, a mournful sound that seemed to signal an end, or perhaps, a beginning. He closed the book gently, the leather cover cool beneath his fingertips.
